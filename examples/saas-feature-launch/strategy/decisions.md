# ADR-001: Per-workspace flat pricing for the dashboards feature

**Status:** Proposed
**Date:** 2026-04-13
**Run:** Gap-Hunter plan run 2026-04-13

## Context

The dashboards feature requires a pricing decision before the announced 2026-05-24 launch. Four candidate models (per-seat, per-workspace, per-aggregated-source, flat-rate) were evaluated. Existing constraints: multi-tenant Stripe-based billing, six-week customer-facing pricing-change notice (contractual), SOC2 audit obligations, six-week implementation timeline.

## Decision

Adopt per-workspace flat pricing for the dashboards feature at launch. Existing customers on per-seat core pricing remain unchanged. The dashboards feature appears as a separate subscription line item per workspace.

## Consequences

- **Positive:** Simplest billing model. Stripe-native — no new infrastructure. Lowest audit surface. Strongest stakeholder alignment (sales, finance, customer success, pilot customers all prefer this or hybrid). Ships in two weeks.
- **Negative:** Pricing inconsistency with per-seat core product creates customer-facing communication burden. Revenue does not scale with deeper team adoption — large workspaces extract disproportionate value relative to small workspaces.
- **Neutral:** Locks the team into this model for at least 8 months (until next pricing review cycle in January 2027).

## Alternatives considered

- **Per-seat:** Aligns with core product but suppresses the network effect the feature creates. Documented friction in deal cycles at peer companies.
- **Per-aggregated-source:** Creates customer cost-management behaviour that undermines feature value. Multiple documented post-mortems. Misses the timeline.
- **Flat-rate bundled:** Leaves revenue on the table. Creates pressure to invent new paid features.
- **Hybrid (workspace + tiered seat caps):** Strong long-term option, but adds three weeks to launch. Reserved as the M-2 escalation path for enterprise tier in the next pricing cycle.

## Adoption-filter check

- **Invariants:** Pass. Multi-tenant RLS preserved. Stripe-native. Audit trail unchanged. SOC2-friendly.
- **Slot-fit:** Existing slot. Workspace creation already triggers a Stripe customer; this extends with a feature-level subscription line item.
- **Pilot-runnable:** Yes. Two-week implementation. One-week pilot with the three design-partner accounts. Rollback path: deactivate the line item at the workspace level.

# ADR-002: Hybrid escalation path documented for January 2027 review

**Status:** Proposed
**Date:** 2026-04-13
**Run:** Gap-Hunter plan run 2026-04-13

## Context

The January 2027 pricing review will revisit dashboards pricing. The chain identified that pure per-workspace pricing constrains the enterprise upgrade path. The team plans to move upmarket in 2026 and needs an explicit hybrid framework reserved for enterprise tier.

## Decision

Document a hybrid pricing framework — workspace-flat for SMB/Mid tier, workspace + tiered seat caps for Enterprise tier — and lock it as the default option for the January 2027 review. No implementation now; documentation only.

## Consequences

- **Positive:** Preserves the upmarket option without committing now. Sales has a clear answer to "can this scale to enterprise contracts?". Pricing committee has a pre-prepared option for the next cycle.
- **Negative:** The framework will need refinement based on actual feature adoption data and customer feedback collected between now and January 2027. Risk of premature commitment if the framework hardens before evidence.
- **Neutral:** No engineering work required at this stage.

## Alternatives considered

- **Defer all enterprise-tier thinking until the next cycle.** Rejected — the chain identified enterprise upgrade path as a Mid-horizon contradiction; deferring without a framework leaves sales unable to answer enterprise questions during deal cycles.
- **Build the hybrid model now alongside workspace-flat.** Rejected — adds three weeks to the launch and we lack WTP data to set the seat caps correctly.

## Adoption-filter check

- **Invariants:** Pass. Pure documentation, no system changes.
- **Slot-fit:** Pricing committee documentation slot.
- **Pilot-runnable:** Not applicable — this is a framework, not an implementation.

# ADR-003: "Value-aligned pricing" definition resolved before final commit

**Status:** Proposed
**Date:** 2026-04-13
**Run:** Gap-Hunter plan run 2026-04-13

## Context

The CEO has stated a preference for "value-aligned pricing" without defining what that means concretely. Every recommendation in this run defers to this undefined constraint. Without resolution, the pricing decision can be re-litigated indefinitely.

## Decision

Schedule a focused conversation with the CEO before the team commits to ADR-001. Outcome must be one of:
1. A concrete definition the team can apply to the pricing decision
2. An explicit deferral acknowledging that "value-aligned" is a long-term aspiration, not a current decision criterion

Document the outcome in this ADR within 5 business days. If no conversation has happened by 2026-04-22, the team proceeds with ADR-001 and notes the deferral.

## Consequences

- **Positive:** Converts an undefined constraint into either a concrete requirement or an acknowledged deferral. Removes the most common source of decision-relitigation.
- **Negative:** May surface a hard constraint that invalidates ADR-001 (low likelihood given the chain's strong convergence).
- **Neutral:** Forces a CEO-level decision that has been deferred for at least three pricing cycles.

## Alternatives considered

- **Proceed without consulting the CEO.** Rejected — the preference has been stated in writing; ignoring it creates internal misalignment.
- **Treat the CEO preference as the primary input and re-run the analysis.** Rejected — the preference is too vague to act on without definition.

## Adoption-filter check

- **Invariants:** Pass. Pure conversation.
- **Slot-fit:** Existing executive-decision-record slot.
- **Pilot-runnable:** Not applicable.
